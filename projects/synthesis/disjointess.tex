
  \item \textbf{Disjointness of the good quantum LDPC code.} $ \ge \Disc$. \footnote{Ours.}

  Denote by $Q_{ab}$ the subset of edges associated multiplicating by $a$ or $a^{-1}$ from left and multiplicating by $b$ or $b^{-1}$ from right namely $ Q_{ab} =  \{ (g, agb), (g, a^{-1}gb^{-1}) \} $, and consider the following edges partiton $Q = \{ Q_{ab} : a,b \in A\times A \}$. By the cleaning lemma \cite{Bravyi_2009}\cite{cleaning_lemm_2}, and the \ctt{enter here, intermidate claim.}, for any logical pauli $g$ there is a represenation $g_{ab}$ such that $g^{\prime}$ acts trivally on the qubits in $Q_{ab}$. Therefore, for any $g$ the set $ A(g) = \{ g_{ab} : a,b \in A \times A \}$  is $ \frac{1}{2}\Delta^{2}-1$-disjointness\footnote{Any qubit belongs to a single $Q_{ab}$ and therefore is not on the support of $g_{ab}$.}. Thus the disjointness of the code is at least: 
  \begin{equation*}
    \begin{split}
      \max_{c} \Delta_{c} \ge \Delta_{\frac{1}{2} \Delta^{2} - 1 } \ge \frac{  |Q|   }{\frac{1}{2} \Delta^{2} - 1  } = \Disc
    \end{split}
  \end{equation*}
