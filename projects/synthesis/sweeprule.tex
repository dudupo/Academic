\section{SWEEP Rule Decoder on the Toric}
The decoder works as follows: each vertex $v$ looks for a suggestion $\hat{v}$ on $\uparrow{(v)}\cap lk_{2}$ that matches $\sigma_{v}^{+}$. If there are more than one, then pick the one which has the minimal weight.

\section{Span Expansion}

Define the potentials functions $L_{i} : \mathbb{R}^{d} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ as follow\footnote{Notice that we use opposite signs relative to Berman and Peter Gacs, yet we agree with Perskil.}: 
\begin{equation*}
  \begin{split}
L_{i}(\xb{}) =    \begin{cases}
  \xb{}_{i} & i < d + 1 \\ 
  - \braket{\mathbf{1}, \xb{}} & i = d+1
\end{cases}
  \end{split}
\end{equation*}
The \textbf{Size} of a subset $S$ is defined as:   
\begin{equation*}
  \begin{split}
  \mathbf{Size}(S) = \sum_{i}\max_{\xb{} \in S} L_{i}(\xb{})
  \end{split}
\end{equation*}
Numerically, we show that the span property holds for the checks. Namely, let $e$ be an unsatisfied check at time $t$, and let $H_{e}$ be the unsatisfied checks at time $t-1$. Then there is a set $H^{\prime}_{e} \subset H_{e}$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item For any $i < d + 1$ there exists $e^{\prime} \in H^{\prime}_{e}$ such $L_{i}(e^{\prime}) \ge L_{i}(e)$. 
  \item There exists $e^{\prime} \in H^{\prime}_{e}$ such that $L_{d+1}(e^{\prime}) \le L_{d+1}(e) + 1 $. 
\end{enumerate}

\begin{proof}
  Consider a vertex $v$ which at time $t$ adjoins to an unsatisfied edge $e$, let $u,w$ be vertices which have a positive faces that adjoin to $e$. 
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item If $\hat{u}|_{e} \neq \emptyset$, then the parallel edge to $e$ going out from $u$ belongs to $H_{e}$. Denote it by $e_u$, taking $e_{u}$ satisfies at least two (three in 4D) of the first $d$ inequalities and the last $d+1$th inequality. Denote by $x \in [d]$ the index of the inequality which isn't satisfied by $e_{u}$. So it remains to prove the existence of $e^{\prime} \in H_{e}$ for which $L_x(e^{\prime}) \ge L_{x}(e)$.

Consider the assignment of bits at time $t-1$ over the complex, and consider the graph $G = \left( \mathcal{F}, E \right)$ induced by associating vertices with any face that is set to $1$ by the assignment. Two vertices are connected if their origin faces share an edge. Let $K \subset \mathcal{F}$ be a connected component of faces that contains the faces supported on $u$.

      We say that $K$ is $d$ far from $u$ if:   
      \begin{equation*}
        \begin{split}
          \max_{f \in K} | f_{x} - u_{x} | \le d
        \end{split}
      \end{equation*}
      Now, we will prove by induction on $d$ that there is a face $f \in K$ with $f_{x} \ge v_{x}$ such one of its edge is not satisfied. 
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item \textbf{Base.} The edge from $v$ which point at the $\hat{z}$ direction must not be satisfied.

        \item \textbf{Assumption.} Assume that for any $K$ which is at most $d$ far from $u$ there is a face, with adjoins edge that is both has a $x$-coordinate that is greater than $u_{x}$ and is not satisfied.   
        \item \textbf{Induction Step.} Notice that if by substitute a stabilizer from $K$ we get $G^{\prime}$ that is at most $d^{\prime}$ far from $u$ for some $d^{\prime} < d$ then we done.  
      \end{enumerate}

    \item  Else, if for any $u$ it holds that $\hat{u}|_{e} = \emptyset$, then it must be that $e \in H_{e}$ because $\hat{v}$ can only decrease the syndrome on $v$. Moreover, it follows that $\hat{v} = \emptyset$ (otherwise, the suggestion would zero out the syndrome on $\uparrow(v) \cap lk_{2}$). Thus, $\sigma_{v} \not\subset \uparrow(v) \cap lk_{2} \Rightarrow$ there is also an edge $e^{\prime} \in H_{e}$ where $e^{\prime} \in \downarrow(v)$. In that case, take $e \in H_{e}$ as the edge satisfies the first $d$ equalities and $e^{\prime}$ for satisfying the $d+1$th inequality.
  \end{enumerate}
